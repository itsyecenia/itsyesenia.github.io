Birthing Options

Your baby's kicks and stretches are a daily reminder that you'll soon get to see your little one's smile. The type of birth experience you want is a very personal decision.

Hospital

The vast majority of women in the U.S. give birth in a hospital. If you have a high-risk pregnancy or want to try having a vaginal birth after a cesarean delivery (VBAC), then a hospital is the safest -- and often the only -- place you can deliver your baby. Even if you have a low-risk pregnancy, you may want to give birth in a hospital where you have ready access to the latest in medical technology.

Pros:

  • Quick access to medical care when needed
  • Available for most any type of birth you desire‚Äîfrom all-natural to VBAC or c-section
  • Insurance covers more expenses
  • Lots of extra supplies to bring home (blankets, pacifiers, pads, diapers, baby brush, etc.)

Cons:

  • Different nurses rotating through your stay
  • Medical intervention is often encouraged and can be hard to refuse
  • Being attached to an IV during labor
  • Sterile atmosphere can stall or affect laboring

Birth Center

Some people consider a birthing center delivery the best of both worlds. The setting is relaxing and home-like, and mind-body support options for childbirth pain such as hypnosis, water births, and doulas are readily available.

Pros:

  • Homey environment is familiar since your prenatal visits are there as well
  • No uncomfortable IV or belly monitors during labor
  • Your birth plan is easily accommodated
  • You‚Äôre encouraged to eat and drink throughout labor

Cons:

  • Insurance may not cover expenses, but it still can end up cheaper
  • May have to transport to hospital if an emergency arises
  • Women with high-risk pregnancies may not be able to give birth at a birthing center
  • Excruciating car ride when in active labor

Home Birth

Only about 2% of women in the U.S. opt for a home birth. It can be a safe and relaxing natural childbirth delivery method for women with a normal, low-risk pregnancy.

Pros:

  • Your own well-known birthing space
  • No need to pack a bag
  • Often prenatal and postnatal visits are in your own home
  • You can be as loud as you need in labor with no fear of disturbing others
